Get excited about Exoplanets!

Welcome to our exoplanet visualiser.


Exoplanet (n): a planet orbiting a star that is not our sun


We are a team of students at Durham University. Exoplanets are one of the most interesting and accessible topics in astrophysics, and have been immortalised in science fiction almost since the genre began. We aim to improve the appeal of data relating to the size, shape, composition and habitability of exoplanets to the wider public by introducing this visualiser tool. Visit the data page to view the visualiser.



RA/DEC coordinate system: Just like we like to use coordinates to describe where a place is on Earth’s surface via longitude and latitude, astronomers use a coordinate system to describe where in the sky we see a cosmological object. Instead of longitude and latitude, astronomers use right ascension (ra) and declination (dec). Ra is the longitude of space, i.e. how far ‘east’ or ‘west’ the object appears. Dec is similar to latitude, i.e. how far up or down an exoplanet appears. See the diagram below for further visualization and clarification. Ra is measured in hours around a circle, with 180° around the circle labelled as 12 hr, and 360° labelled as 24 hours. Dec is measured in degrees from the equator.
